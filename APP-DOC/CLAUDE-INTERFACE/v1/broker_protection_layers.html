behavioral camouflage — don't look like a winning algo
B1WIN RATE MANAGEMENTCRITICAL▶
Problem: Win rates above ~65% trigger broker profitability reviews. High win rate + consistent PnL = "looks like front-running or latency arb" to compliance.

Solution: Intentionally allow some trades to expire at small losses. SURGE should include a "deliberate noise" module — a configurable loss-injection rate targeting 55-62% net win rate at the account level. True alpha is in expectancy (R-multiple), not raw win rate.

Never exceed 65% win rateTarget 55-62%High R-multiple compensates

Implementation: Add a parameter NOISE_RATE=0.08 — on 8% of signals, submit an intentionally poor-entry version (wrong side of spread, slightly late). These are "paid camouflage losses."
B2HOLD TIME DISTRIBUTIONHIGH▶
Problem: Scalping algos (consistent 30-90s holds) are often auto-flagged or TOS-banned at prop firms. Identical hold times = robot fingerprint.

Solution: Use lognormal hold-time distribution. Mean = your true target, sigma = 0.4. Some trades hold 2 minutes, some 40 minutes, even if all triggered the same signal. This mimics discretionary trader behavior.

No consistent hold timesLognormal distributionMix scalp + swing on same account

Max hold rule: Ensure ≥15% of trades exceed 4 hours hold time. Even if you exit early, open a hedge. Looks like you "managed" the position.
B3INSTRUMENT ROTATIONHIGH▶
Problem: Trading only NQ or only ES on a prop account makes your equity curve "too clean" — correlated, consistent, easily identified.

Solution: Rotate instruments. Even if SURGE only generates strong signals on NQ, allocate 20% of trades to correlated instruments (ES, RTY, YM). Accept slightly lower performance — you're buying camouflage.

Rotate 3-4 instruments minimumInclude some losers
B4SESSION VARIATIONMEDIUM▶
Problem: Algos that only trade 9:30-11am EST every day are trivially identifiable by session analysis.

Solution: SURGE session mask: run primary signals in optimal session, but add ghost orders (0.1x size) in London open, Asia open. These lose money on average but break the session-pattern fingerprint. Cost: small drag. Benefit: undetectable session profile.
B5DRAWDOWN THEATERMEDIUM▶
Problem: Zero-drawdown months look impossible to risk teams. Suspicious perfect equity curves get reviewed.

Solution: Allow 1-3 "theatrical drawdown" days per month — reduce position size to 10% of normal and trade through a known losing condition deliberately. Adds realism. Keeps equity curve looking like a skilled human, not a locked-in arb.

front-run / B-book / last-look defense
F1B-BOOK DETECTIONDETECT▶
What it is: B-book brokers take the other side of your trade. When you win, they lose. Profitable traders get manually moved to worse fill conditions or restricted.

Detection: Log exact requested price vs fill price for 200+ trades. Calculate mean slippage. B-book signature: positive slippage (you get worse than requested) on winners, neutral/negative slippage on losers. Run t-test.

Log every fillCompare req vs fillAsymmetric slippage = B-book

Counter: Move to ECN/STP broker (IBKR, Tradeovate, Rithmic-connected NinjaTrader). Pay commission explicitly — you want a broker who makes money from commission, not from your losses.
F2LAST-LOOK DEFENSECOUNTER▶
What it is: Some FX/CFD brokers use "last look" — they review your order for 50-200ms before filling. If your order is directionally correct (price moved your way), they reject it.

Counter: Only use exchange-cleared futures (CME, CBOT, ICE). No last-look possible on exchange. NQ/ES futures via Rithmic or CQG have true exchange fills — no broker intermediary.

Avoid FX spot for algoAvoid CFDs entirelyCME futures = no last-look
F3ORDER FLOW CAMOUFLAGEPREVENT▶
What it is: Brokers/prime brokers can see your pending orders and systematically front-run large or predictable entries.

Counter — Iceberg + jitter stack:
— Never place stops at obvious round numbers (broker algos hunt these)
— Use iceberg orders: show 10% of size, hide 90%
— Randomize order submission time ±3-7 seconds post-signal
— Split large orders into 3-5 child orders across 30-120 second window (TWAP-like)
— Vary order type: sometimes LMT, sometimes stop-limit (disguises pattern)

Iceberg ordersTWAP child-order splitAvoid round-number stops
F4BROKER TOXICITY SCOREMONITOR▶
What it is: Ongoing systematic measurement of broker behavior to detect degradation before account action.

Metrics to track (daily):
— Mean fill latency (ms) — sudden increase = flagged
— Slippage asymmetry ratio (winners vs losers)
— Requote rate (%)
— Spread vs published at time of fill
— Withdrawal processing time (days)

Trigger: Any metric drifts >2 standard deviations from 30-day baseline → escalate to "broker degradation" alert → begin capital migration to backup broker.
escalation playbook — what to do when things go wrong
!
ACCOUNT RESTRICTED (trading halted)
Broker limits new positions but holds existing.
↳ Immediately hedge equivalent exposure at backup broker. Do not close existing (may be profitable). Contact broker via entity email — request written reason. Document everything. Do NOT agree verbally to anything.
!
ACCOUNT CLOSED — funds held
Broker terminates and freezes withdrawal.
↳ Request written closure reason within 48hr. If regulated broker (FCA/CFTC), file regulatory complaint immediately — this creates leverage. Consult trading attorney. Funds legally held >30 days without cause = regulatory violation in most jurisdictions.
↳ Prevention: never hold >60 days profit in broker account. Sweep weekly.
!
PROP FIRM RULE BREACH NOTICE
Daily loss limit approached, or consistency rule flagged.
↳ SURGE should have a real-time rule monitor. On 80% of daily loss limit: auto-stop all new entries. On 90%: close all positions. Never let the firm's system liquidate — you get worse fills and a note on account.
!
WITHDRAWAL DELAYED
Broker "processing" withdrawal for >5 business days.
↳ Escalate to compliance department in writing. Reference regulatory standards. In parallel: reduce new deposits, begin capital migration. A broker delaying withdrawals from profitable traders = structural red flag. Exit relationship within 30 days.
!
FRONT-RUN PATTERN DETECTED
Toxicity score breach — slippage asymmetry confirmed.
↳ Do not confront broker. Begin quiet capital migration over 2-3 weeks (sudden full withdrawal triggers review). Run lower size while migrating. Complete exit before broker notices reduced activity.

# toxicity.py — slippage asymmetry detector import numpy as np from scipy import stats class ToxicityMonitor: def log_fill(self, req_px, fill_px, direction, pnl): """direction: 1=long, -1=short""" slip = (fill_px - req_px) * direction self.slippage.append({ "slip": slip, "winner": pnl > 0 }) def score(self) -> dict: winners = [x["slip"] for x in self.slippage if x["winner"]] losers = [x["slip"] for x in self.slippage if not x["winner"]] t, p = stats.ttest_ind(winners, losers) return { "winner_slip_mean": np.mean(winners), "loser_slip_mean": np.mean(losers), "asymmetry_pval": p, "b_book_flag": p < 0.05 and np.mean(winners) > np.mean(losers) }
