Based on the interview with David Hanlin (Laptop Legend), here are the subtle rules for algorithmic trading and the structured inputs for AI extraction regarding entries, exits, and scaling.

### **1. Entry Rules & Alpha Extraction**
* **The "Tightness" Principle:** The most critical entry signal is when price action becomes "very tight" [[16:57](http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=PBhrBnrtbxs&t=1017)]. This coiling often precedes a major expansion.
* **Pattern Preferences:** * **Ascending Support + Flat Resistance:** High probability for an upside breakout [[15:02](http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=PBhrBnrtbxs&t=902)].
    * **Descending Resistance + Flat Support:** High probability for a downside breakdown [[15:07](http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=PBhrBnrtbxs&t=907)].
* **Indicator Convergence:** For "bigger picture" trades, wait for EMAs or SMAs to catch up to the price, ensuring the trade is "coiled" before execution [[15:45](http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=PBhrBnrtbxs&t=945)].
* **Time as a Filter:** Avoid positioning too early in a flag or range if the structure "needs more time" to develop; early entry often leads to being chopped out by noise [[16:19](http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=PBhrBnrtbxs&t=979)].

### **2. Scaling Decisions: The Pyramiding Protocol**
Hanlin’s "alpha" comes from sacrificing win rate for massive risk-to-reward via aggressive pyramiding [[04:24](http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=PBhrBnrtbxs&t=264)].
* **Risk Recycling:** Do not simply add more risk. Instead, move the stop-loss of the initial position to a "tighter" structural point once the trade moves in your favor [[05:16](http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=PBhrBnrtbxs&t=316)].
* **The Math of Scaling:** If the stop is moved to break-even or a tighter structural level, you can add a new position so that the *total dollar risk* remains the same as the original risk (e.g., $10k), but your total position size is now much larger [[05:22](http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=PBhrBnrtbxs&t=322)].
* **Compounding Results:** This method can turn a standard $10k risk into a $1 million gain by iteratively moving stops and adding size as new structures develop [[05:54](http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=PBhrBnrtbxs&t=354)].


### **3. Exit & Trade Management Rules**
* **Set and Forget:** Once the decision is made and the risk is defined, you must "see it through" [[01:41](http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=PBhrBnrtbxs&t=101)].
* **Moving Stops Correctly:** Moving a stop to break-even prematurely is a sign of not trusting your analysis [[01:36](http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=PBhrBnrtbxs&t=96)]. Stops should only be moved based on **market structure**, not PnL [[05:09](http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=PBhrBnrtbxs&t=309)].
* **Win Rate vs. Profit Factor:** Hanlin operates with a sub-50% win rate (approx. 41%) [[04:02](http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=PBhrBnrtbxs&t=242)]. An algorithm or AI should be optimized for **average win size** relative to **average loss**, rather than purely maximizing win frequency [[04:29](http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=PBhrBnrtbxs&t=269)].

### **4. Structured Inputs for AI Training**
To extract alpha from Hanlin's style, an AI model should ingest the following data points:
* **Volatility Contraction:** Delta between price ranges over the last X bars (searching for the "coiling" effect) [[15:50](http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=PBhrBnrtbxs&t=950)].
* **Support/Resistance Slope:** Quantitative measurement of whether support is ascending (bullish) or resistance is descending (bearish) [[15:02](http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=PBhrBnrtbxs&t=902)].
* **Moving Average Proximity:** Distance between current price and key EMAs/SMAs [[15:45](http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=PBhrBnrtbxs&t=945)].
* **Structural Stop Points:** Identification of the most recent "higher low" or "lower high" to trigger automated stop-loss adjustments for pyramiding [[05:39](http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=PBhrBnrtbxs&t=339)].
